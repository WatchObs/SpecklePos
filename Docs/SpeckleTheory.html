Speckle Size and Motion in Near and Far Fields (Red Laser)

This document summarizes speckle size and motion in the near field and far field, and includes explicit computation of the near→far transition distance for a red laser.

1. Far-field speckle size

In the far field, the illuminated spot of diameter L acts as a coherent aperture. The mean speckle grain size at sensor distance z is:

dfar ≈ ( λ · z ) / L
  • λ = wavelength (m)
  • z = distance from scattering surface to sensor (m)
  • L = illuminated spot diameter (m)

2. Near-field speckle size

In the near field, speckle statistics are governed by local phase curvature. The mean speckle size scales as:

dnear ≈ √( λ · z )
  • λ = wavelength (m)
  • z = distance from scattering surface to sensor (m)

3. Near → Far Field Transition

The boundary between near-field and far-field speckle is given by:

ztransition = L² / λ

with:

  • L² / λ = characteristic distance where the illuminated spot behaves like a diffraction aperture.
Near field: z ≪ L² / λ
Far field: z ≫ L² / λ

4. Red Laser Example (λ ≈ 650 nm)

For a typical red laser:

  • λ = 650 nm = 6.5 × 10⁻⁷ m

Let the illuminated spot diameter be:

  • L = 5 mm = 5 × 10⁻³ m

Compute the near→far transition distance:

ztransition = L² / λ
= (5 × 10⁻³ m)² / (6.5 × 10⁻⁷ m)
= 25 × 10⁻⁶ / 6.5 × 10⁻⁷ ≈ 38 m
Conclusion: For a 5 mm red spot, any sensor distance much less than ~38 m is near-field. Telescope optical paths (≈0.5–2 m) are therefore firmly in the near-field regime.

5. Speckle Motion Scaling

Speckle motion sensitivity scales roughly as:

Δxspeckle ∝ z / dspeckle

Near field (using dnear ≈ √(λz)):

Δxnear ∝ √( z / λ )

Far field (using dfar ≈ λz/L):

Δxfar ∝ L / λ
Near field: motion grows with √z and is very sensitive to distance.
Far field: motion is controlled mainly by aperture size L and wavelength λ.
